The Content Matrix skill generates a month of LinkedIn post ideas in a single table by crossing your content pillars against eight proven content formats. Based on the Justin Welsh content matrix method, every cell in the table is a specific post idea — not a theme or a vague suggestion. Each idea is tailored to the exact combination of pillar and format it sits in. The output is a markdown table you can copy into Notion and use immediately.

## Trigger phrases

```
give me post ideas
content matrix
what should I post about
generate post ideas
content ideation
map out my content for the month
```

## Prerequisites

No API keys required. The skill uses `about-me.md` and `voice.md` if they exist. Without them, it asks for your background and pillars directly.

<Tip>
  If `about-me.md` exists in your project, the skill reads it and pre-fills your context automatically, then tells you what it pulled. You only need to confirm or adjust your pillars.
</Tip>

## Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather inputs">
    The skill checks for `about-me.md`. If found, it pre-fills your background and skips that question.

    It then asks for your content pillars. Three options:

    - **Type them** — provide 3–5 pillars of your own
    - **Pull from voice.md** — use topics already defined in your voice files
    - **Suggest them** — the skill reads `about-me.md`, proposes 4 pillars covering your positioning, and asks you to confirm or edit before continuing

    Minimum 3 pillars. Maximum 5. Fewer than 3 produces a matrix that is too narrow; more than 5 dilutes it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the matrix">
    The skill generates a table with your pillars as rows and the 8 content formats as columns. Every cell contains one specific, concrete post idea. No idea is reused across different pillars.

    If `voice.md` exists, the language of every idea is tuned to match your voice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Output and highlight">
    The full table is output in a markdown code block for easy copying into Notion. Below the table, the skill names the single strongest idea across the entire matrix and explains why.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Offer the next move">
    The skill asks if you want to turn any cell into a full post using the Post Writer or Post Formatter skill, referenced by cell position (e.g. "Pillar 2 / Contrarian").
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The eight content formats

These eight formats always appear as columns, always in this order:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Actionable" icon="check">
    Ultra-specific how-to. Teaches the reader to do one thing. No theory — just the steps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Motivational" icon="heart">
    Inspirational story about someone who did something extraordinary in the niche.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Analytical" icon="chart-bar">
    A breakdown of why something works the way it does. Data, logic, and reasoning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Contrarian" icon="arrow-left-right">
    Goes against the common advice in the niche and backs it up. The best performing format for polarising your audience in a useful direction.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Observation" icon="eye">
    A hidden, silent, or underdiscussed trend the author has noticed. First-person perspective is essential.
  </Card>
  <Card title="X vs Y" icon="scale-balanced">
    A comparison of two entities: tools, styles, frameworks, companies, or approaches.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Present vs Future" icon="clock">
    Current state vs a specific prediction, with the reasoning behind the shift.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Listicle" icon="list">
    A list of resources, tips, mistakes, lessons, or steps. Specific number in the headline.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What a good cell looks like

Each cell contains a specific headline idea, not a theme:

| | Actionable | Contrarian | Listicle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn growth** | The 3-line hook formula I use to open every high-performing post | Stop optimising your posting time — consistency matters more than timing | 7 LinkedIn posts that got me 10k followers (and what made them work) |
| **AI tools** | How to use Claude to turn a 1-hour interview into 5 posts in 20 minutes | AI will not replace copywriters — it will replace copywriters who use it badly | 5 AI tools that save me 8 hours a week (and 2 I deleted after a month) |

<Warning>
  "AI tools" is not a post idea. "How to use Claude to turn a 1-hour interview into 5 posts in 20 minutes" is a post idea. The skill enforces specificity in every cell.
</Warning>

## Rules and constraints

- Minimum 3 pillars, maximum 5
- Every cell idea must be specific to that pillar AND that format — no reusing the same idea across pillars
- Language is tuned to `voice.md` if the file exists
- British English unless `voice.md` specifies American
- No em dashes
